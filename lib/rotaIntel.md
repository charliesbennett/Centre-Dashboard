# UKLC Rota Intelligence Document
## For Claude Code: Automated Rota Generation

---

## 1. ORGANISATION OVERVIEW

UKLC (UK Language Courses Ltd) runs residential and homestay English language and activity programmes for international students aged 8-17 at boarding schools and university campuses across the UK, primarily during July and August.

Each centre runs on a rolling intake model where different groups arrive and depart on different dates across the summer. The programme for each group follows a repeating weekly structure, but because groups overlap with staggered arrivals, the centre is always running multiple groups at different points in their programme simultaneously.

Centres can range from approximately 30 students (e.g. Queenswood Hatfield: ~99 students across the summer) to 350+ students (e.g. NRC Nantwich: ~360 students, QMU London: ~370 students). Staff numbers scale with student numbers based on the ratios described below.

---

## 2. PROGRAMME TYPES

UKLC runs two types of timetable at its centres:

### Zig-Zag (ZZ) Programme
Students are divided into two cohorts (Group A and Group B). Group A has English lessons in the morning and activities/excursions in the afternoon, while Group B has the reverse. This effectively doubles the use of classroom and activity spaces. In practice, in the Programmes spreadsheet, you can see some groups have "English Test AM / Multi-Activity PM" on the same day while other groups have "Multi-Activity AM / English Test PM". This is the hallmark of ZZ.

### Non-Zig-Zag (NZZ) Programme
All students have English lessons in the morning (9:00-12:30 approximately) and activities/excursions in the afternoon (2:00pm-5:30pm approximately).

### Which Centres Use Which Type
- **London centres** (QMU, KCL, Kingston): Typically ZZ
- **Other centres**: Can be either ZZ or NZZ - it is not yet confirmed which centres will be ZZ or NZZ for 2026
- From the 2025 rotas: Nantwich (ZZ), Queenswood (NZZ), Winchester (NZZ), QMU London (ZZ)

### Key Difference for Rota Planning
- **NZZ**: Teachers are always needed in the morning. Activity staff are always needed in the afternoon. Evenings are shared.
- **ZZ**: Both teachers and activity staff may be needed in both morning AND afternoon slots, because lessons and activities run simultaneously in both halves of the day.

## 3. THE WEEKLY PROGRAMME STRUCTURE

### Typical 2-Week Programme for a Group (from real 2025 data)

**Week 1:**

| Saturday | English Test / Multi-Activity* | Multi-Activity / English Test* | Disco / Film Night |

| Monday | English Lessons / Nantwich Orientation* | Nantwich Orientation / English Lessons* | Dragons Den |
| Tuesday | English Lessons / Vlogging* | Vlogging / English Lessons* | Karaoke |
| Wednesday | English Lessons / Excursion (Chester)* | Excursion (Chester) / English Lessons* | Paparazzi |
| Thursday | English Lessons / Multi-Activity* | Multi-Activity / English Lessons* | Flag Ceremony |

**Week 2:**
| Day | AM | PM | Evening |
|-----|----|----|---------|
| Friday | Multi-Activity / English Lessons* | English Lessons / Multi-Activity* | Talent Show |
| Saturday | Full-Day Excursion (e.g. Stratford) | Full-Day Excursion (cont.) | Evening Ent |
| Sunday | Free Time / Optional Excursion | Activities | Oscars Night |

| Thursday | Depart | Depart | Awards (if last night) |

*In ZZ centres, the AM/PM activities are swapped for the two cohorts. In NZZ centres, English Lessons are always AM and activities always PM.

### Key Programme Pattern
- **Arrival day**: No lessons. Airport pickup, welcome, orientation.
- **Day after arrival**: English placement test (counts as a teaching session in the rota)
- **2 half-day excursions per week** (e.g. Chester, Shrewsbury, Nantwich Town)
- **1 full-day excursion per week** (e.g. Liverpool, Stratford, Manchester, London, Cambridge, Canterbury)
- **Evening entertainment every night** (Disco, Quiz, Film Night, Karaoke, Paparazzi, Dragons Den, Talent Show, Trashion Show, Oscars, Flag Ceremony, Murder Mystery, Scavenger Hunt, Conversation Club, Sports Day, Awards)
- **Departure day**: Airport transfers, no lessons. Centre shutdown for final groups.

### Daily Timings
- **08:00**: Breakfast
- **08:30**: 30-minute Morning Meeting (staff)
- **09:00-12:30**: Morning sessions (English Lessons or Activities depending on programme type)
- **12:30**: Lunch
- **14:00-17:30**: Afternoon sessions (Activities, Excursions, or English Lessons in ZZ)
- **18:00**: Dinner
- **19:30/20:00-22:00**: Evening Entertainment
- **22:00-23:00**: Students' Bedtime (varies by age - see Section 9)

### Session Definition
- **1 session = approximately 3.5 hours**
- A morning of teaching = 1 session
- An afternoon of activities = 1 session
- An evening entertainment = 1 session
- A half-day excursion = 1 session
- A **full-day excursion = 2 sessions** (AM + PM)
- An airport transfer = 1 or 2 sessions (depending on duration)
- Testing (placement tests on day after arrival) = counts as teaching sessions
- "Admin" = counts as a session when assigned
- "Dinner" supervision = counts as a session when assigned
- If a session is 3+ hours, staff get a 30-minute break (or two 15-minute breaks)

### English Plus / Chosen Programme Options
During activity slots, students may be in one of these programmes:
- **Multi-Activity**: General sports and activities
- **Football**: Specialist football coaching (delivered by Football Specialists + SAIs)
- **Performing Arts / Drama**: Specialist performing arts (delivered by PA Specialists)
- **Intensive English**: Additional English lessons (delivered by TALs - labelled "Int Eng" in rotas)
- **Vlogging Workshop**: Video production activity

## 4. ALL ROLES AND THEIR ROTA RULES

### How Management Roles Appear in the Rota
From real 2025 rotas, management roles (CM, CD, EAM, SWC) are tracked differently from frontline staff:
- Their "Sessions to work" is set to **0** in the rota spreadsheet (they are salaried, not session-counted)
- Their actual sessions appear in the grid as "Office" for most slots, with occasional participation in activities, excursions, evening entertainment, and airport transfers
- At smaller centres (e.g. Queenswood), management may show sessions counted (24) and regularly exceed their target (e.g. CM showing 29 sessions against a 24 target)

### 4.1 Management Roles

#### Centre Manager (CM)
- **Working hours**: Up to 60 hours/week, 6-day working week (1 day off per week)
- **Rota tracking**: Not session-counted (B=0 in rota). Shown as "Office" for most slots.
- **Teaches**: Only if qualified and if necessary (emergency cover)
- **Excursions**: Does not normally lead excursions (oversees from centre)
- **Evening Entertainment**: Oversees; occasionally participates (e.g. "Attractions", "Karaoke")
- **Airport Duty**: Oversees organisation at centre; does not normally do airport runs
- **Day off**: Any day of the week; when CM is off, CD or EAM becomes Duty Manager
- **Duty Manager rota**: Part of overnight duty rotation with CD and EAM (carries duty phone)
- **Real example**: At Queenswood 2025, CM Michelle Vera was logged as "Office" for nearly all AM+PM slots, with occasional "MultiActiv" and "Attractions" evening duties.

#### Course Director (CD)
- **Working hours**: Up to 50 hours/week, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: Not session-counted (B=0 in rota). Shown as "Office" for most slots.
- **Teaches**: At least 3 sessions per week. In practice, CD often teaches when short-staffed. At Queenswood 2025, CD Nevra had "Teach" sessions alongside "Office" duties.
- **Excursions**: Can accompany but primarily manages academics
- **Evening Entertainment**: Can supervise (e.g. "Karaoke", "Talent Show" in Queenswood rota)
- **Airport Duty**: Can assist with meet and greet
- **Day off**: Any day (coordinates with CM to ensure management cover)
- **Duty Manager rota**: Part of overnight duty rotation

#### Excursion and Activity Manager (EAM)
- **Working hours**: Management role, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: Not session-counted (B=0 in rota). Shown as "Office" for most slots.
- **Teaches**: No
- **Excursions**: Plans, prepares, and oversees; leads excursions when needed. At Queenswood 2025, EAM Bethan had multiple "Excursion" entries in her rota alongside Office duties.
- **Evening Entertainment**: Plans and leads (e.g. "Dragons Den", "Karaoke", "Sports Day", "Talent Show")
- **Airport Duty**: Assists as required
- **Day off**: Any day (coordinates with CM/CD for management cover)

#### Safeguarding and Welfare Coordinator (SWC)
- **Working hours**: Management-level role, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: Not session-counted at larger centres. At Queenswood 2025, SWC Eva had B=24, C=22.
- **Teaches**: Can teach if qualified
- **Excursions**: Conducts pre-departure checks; leads excursions and transfers as needed. At Queenswood 2025, SWC had "TRANSFER", "Excursion", and "Office" assignments.
- **Evening Entertainment**: On duty as required (e.g. "Oscars", "Games night")
- **Airport Duty**: Handles transfers when needed
- **Day off**: Any day (coordinates with management team)
- **Special note**: Not at all centres. Present at centres with larger student numbers. Devises mealtime and evening supervision rotas.

### 4.2 Teaching Roles

#### Standard Full-Time Teacher (FTT)
- **Sessions**: 22 sessions per fortnight (6-day working week)
- **Rota tracking**: B=22 in rota spreadsheet
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Teaching AM + Teaching PM on most weekdays (in NZZ, this means 2 teaching sessions per day). On ZZ programmes, still teaches 2 sessions per day but may teach AM one group and PM another group.
- **Extra sessions**: The 2 "extra" sessions beyond pure teaching come from: evening entertainment (e.g. "Quiz", "Awards", "Disco"), testing on arrival weekends, or occasional excursion duty.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Mark Ryan (FTT, B=22) - Testing Sat AM+PM, then Teaching Mon-Fri AM+PM for week 1. Quiz evening x1, Awards evening x1 = 22 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: FTTs teach AM+PM on teaching days. They do NOT normally do activities or excursions (except as their 2 extra sessions per fortnight). Their extra sessions are typically evening entertainment or a single excursion.
- **Teaches**: YES - primary duty
- **Excursions**: Rarely - only as part of 2 extra sessions. In Nantwich W3&4, some FTTs had "Chester" (1 excursion session) as one of their extras.
- **Evening Entertainment**: 1-2 per fortnight (Quiz, Awards, Disco, Film Night typically)
- **Airport Duty**: Occasionally (counts as 1-2 sessions). In Nantwich W3&4, some FTTs had "airport" sessions.
- **Day off**: 1 day off per week. In practice from real rotas, FTTs appear to NOT have sessions on certain days (gaps in the grid = their day off, though not explicitly marked "OFF").

#### 5 Day Full-Time Teacher (5-Day FTT)
- **Sessions**: Works over 5 days only (typically Monday-Friday)
- **Teaches**: YES - primary and only duty
- **Excursions**: NO
- **Evening Entertainment**: NO
- **Airport Duty**: NO
- **Day off**: Weekends off - goes home at end of each day
- **Special note**: London-based non-residential role only.

#### Teacher and Activity Leader (TAL)
- **Sessions**: 22 sessions per fortnight
- **Rota tracking**: B=22 in rota spreadsheet (same as FTT)
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Mix of Teaching, Intensive English ("Int Eng"), activities, excursions, and evening entertainment. Much more varied than FTT schedules.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Eve Johnson (TAL, B=22) - Setup eve, Int English Sat AM+PM, Film night eve, Teaching Mon AM, Teaching Tue AM, Karaoke eve, Teaching Wed AM, Chester PM, Teaching Thu AM, Int Eng PM, Teaching Fri PM, Stratford AM+PM (FDE), Admin Mon, teach (cover) Tue AM+PM, Scav Hunt eve, teach (cover) PM, Activities Fri AM, Admin PM, Awards eve = 22 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: TALs alternate between teaching and activity delivery within the same fortnight. They are the "Swiss army knife" role. On a given day, a TAL might teach AM and do Intensive English PM, or teach AM and lead an excursion PM.
- **Teaches**: YES
- **Excursions**: YES - leads excursions (Chester, Stratford, Liverpool etc.)
- **Evening Entertainment**: YES (Karaoke, Film night, Scavenger Hunt, etc.)
- **Airport Duty**: YES when required
- **Day off**: 1 day per week, typically a weekday

### 4.3 Activity Roles

#### Activity Coordinator (AC) / Sport Coordinator (SC)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: B=24 in rota spreadsheet
- **Note**: AC and SC are functionally equivalent in the rota (same session count, same types of assignments). SC focuses on sports delivery while AC focuses on general activity coordination.

#### Activity Leader (AL)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: B=24 in rota spreadsheet
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Activities, excursions, evening entertainment, vlogging, dinner supervision. NO teaching.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Lucy Ainsworth (AL, B=24) - Setup, dinner, Liverpool FDE, Nantwich AM+PM, Vlogging AM+PM, Paparazzi eve, Activities AM+PM, Stratford FDE, Oscars eve, Chester PM, Trashion eve, Vlogging PM, Shrewsbury PM = 24 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: ALs do a wide mix of activities, excursions, and evening entertainment. They never teach. They are assigned "dinner" supervision slots. They do Vlogging workshops.

#### Sport and Activity Instructor (SAI)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight, 6-day working week
- **Rota tracking**: B=24 in rota spreadsheet
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Very similar to AL but with more sports-specific sessions. Also does airport pickups, excursions, evening entertainment, dinner supervision.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Elizabeth Metge (SAI, B=24) - dinner, welcome, pickup, activities, Liverpool FDE, Nantwich PM, Dragons eve, Vlogging AM, Activities PM, Talent eve, Stratford FDE, airport runs, Chester FDE, dinner, Shrewsbury PM, Activities PM, Disco eve = 24 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: SAIs get more "dinner" and "welcome" assignments on arrival days. They do airport runs more frequently than other roles.

#### London Activity Leader (LAL) / London Activity Coordinator (LAC)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight, 6-day working week
- **Session times**: 8:45am-1:30pm, 2pm-6:45pm, 7:30pm-11pm
- **Special note**: London-specific roles for QMU, KCL, and Kingston centres.

### 4.4 Welfare Roles

#### House Parent (HP)
- **Working hours**: Up to 40 hours/week
- **Rota tracking**: B=22 in rota spreadsheet (from Queenswood 2025 data)
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Mix of Office, MultiActiv, Excursions, evening entertainment, and transfer duties. NOT teaching.
- **Real example (Queenswood 2025)**: House Parent Isabella (B=22, C=21) - MultiActiv PM, Office AM, Excursion AM+PM, Paparazzi eve, Office AM, Oscars eve, Excursion AM+PM, Office AM+AM, MultiActiv PM, Disco eve, Excursion AM+PM, Office AM, Games night eve, MultiActiv PM, Talent Show eve, Excursion AM+PM, Transfer = 21 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: House Parents do a lot of excursion duty and office/admin work alongside pastoral care. They participate in evening entertainment. They are assigned transfer duties on departure days.
- **Only at**: Bristol (Clifton College) and Queenswood (Hatfield) - confirmed for 2026.

### 4.5 Specialist Roles

#### Football Specialist (when combined with residential role)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight when combined with AL/SAI role (residential)
- **Sessions**: 6-12 per fortnight when standalone (non-residential)
- **Rota tracking**: B=24 when residential
- **Primary pattern in real rotas**: Football sessions, Football Excursions ("FB Excur"), general activities, excursions, and evening entertainment.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Callum Morrison (Football, B=24) - setup, pickup, dinner, activities, Football PM, Liverpool FDE, Nantwich AM, Football PM x multiple, Flag eve, Football AM, FB Excur AM+PM (e.g. Etihad Stadium), Football AM, field eve, Trashion eve, Football, room inspection = 24 sessions.
- **Key pattern**: Football specialists deliver football in PM slots (matching the English Plus programme slot) and do FB Excur (football-specific excursions to stadiums). They also participate in general activities, evening ents, and airport duties when needed.

#### Performing Arts / Drama Specialist (when combined with residential role)
- **Sessions**: 24 sessions per fortnight when combined with AL/SAI role
- **Rota tracking**: B=24 when residential
- **Primary pattern**: Drama sessions in AM and/or PM, excursions, evening entertainment.
- **Real example (NRC Nantwich 2025)**: Marcia Lopes Tavares (Drama, B=24) - dinner, welcome, Drama PM, Liverpool FDE, Nantwich PM, Drama PM, Karaoke eve, Activities AM, Drama PM, Drama AM, Manchester FDE, Stratford FDE, Oscars eve, Drama AM+PM, Vlogging PM, Shrewsbury PM, Drama PM, Disco eve = 24 sessions.

## 5. SUPERVISION RATIOS

### 5.1 Class Size
- **Target class size: 16 students per class**
- Can go to 17 or 18 if absolutely needed, but this should be avoided
- Each class requires one qualified teacher (FTT, TAL, or CD)
- Number of classes = ceil(student_count / 16)
- Number of teachers needed = number of classes running simultaneously

### 5.2 Residential Accommodation Ratios
- **Students aged 12 and over**: Minimum 1 residential adult : 20 students
- **Students under 12**: Minimum 1 residential adult : 15 students

### 5.3 Excursion Ratios
- **Minimum staff per excursion**: 2 UKLC team members (one designated as Excursion Leader)
- **General ratio**: 1:20 for students aged 12+
- **Students under 12**: 1:15
- **Students aged 8-10**: 1:10
- **Group leaders** can be included in ratios BUT only in relation to their own group

### 5.4 Evening Entertainment & Mealtime Ratios
- Follow the general supervision ratios (1:20 for 12+, 1:15 for under 12)
- Sufficient staff must be on duty for mealtime supervision
- A clearly laid out rota must exist for evening/bedtime supervision

### 5.5 Airport Transfer Ratios
- Minimum 2 adults for any airport transfer (1 UKLC team member + can include group leader)
- Drivers carrying young people must be over 21 and DBS-checked

### Ratio Calculation Formula
```
teachers_needed = ceil(total_students_in_lessons / 16)
excursion_staff_needed = max(2, ceil(students_on_excursion / ratio_denominator))
```

## 6. ARRIVAL AND DEPARTURE DAY RULES (from real 2025 rotas)

### Arrival Day

**What happens in the rota:**
From the real rotas, arrival day assignments for non-management staff include:
- "pickup" / "airport" / "Airport Run" - going to the airport
- "welcome" - greeting students at centre
- "dinner" - supervising first dinner
- "setup" - preparing the centre
- "Arrival [Group Name]" - specifically assigned to meet a named group (Queenswood format)
- Evening entertainment runs as normal (e.g. "Disco", "Film night", "welcome" entertainment)

**Who does what:**
- **SAIs and ALs**: Most airport pickups fall to SAIs and ALs. They are assigned "pickup", "airport", "dinner", "welcome" duties.
- **Football staff**: Also do pickups and setup on arrival day.
- **FTTs**: Generally NOT assigned arrival day duties (they start with Testing the following day). Occasionally assigned "setup" evening.
- **TALs**: May do setup but often start with "Int English" the day after arrival.
- **Management**: "Office" - coordinating from centre.

**Do lessons run on arrival day?**
- **NO** - there are no English lessons on arrival day.
- **Testing happens the day AFTER arrival** (e.g. if groups arrive Friday, testing is Saturday). Testing is counted as teaching sessions for FTTs.

**Important: Rolling arrivals**
In practice, not all groups arrive on the same day. Groups arrive across different dates (some Friday, some Sunday, some the following week). Airport transfer duties are spread across multiple days, not just one single "arrival day". The Programmes sheet shows each group's individual arrival date.

### Departure Day

**What happens in the rota:**
- "airport" / "Transfer" - taking students to the airport
- "close down" - centre shutdown/teardown for final groups
- FTTs may teach a shortened final morning session before departure (e.g. "English Lessons 9am-11am")
- "Awards" evening on the last night before departure

## 7. DAY OFF RULES (from real 2025 rotas)

### How Days Off Appear in the Rota
Days off are NOT explicitly marked as "OFF" in the spreadsheet. Instead, a staff member simply has **no entries** for that day's AM, PM, and Eve columns. The session counter in column C tracks whether they've been assigned enough sessions.

### Observed Patterns from Real Rotas

| Role | Sessions/Fortnight | Approximate Days Off per Fortnight | Pattern |
|------|-------------------|-----------------------------------|---------|
| CM | Salaried (B=0) | ~2 (1 per week) | Gaps in "Office" entries |
| CD | Salaried (B=0) | ~2 (1 per week) | Gaps in "Office" entries |
| EAM | Salaried (B=0) | ~2 (1 per week) | Gaps in "Office" entries |
| SWC | B=24 or salaried | ~2 (1 per week) | Mixed Office + activities |
| FTT | B=22 | ~2-3 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| TAL | B=22 | ~2-3 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| AL | B=24 | ~2 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| SAI | B=24 | ~2 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| HP | B=22 | ~2-3 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| Football (res) | B=24 | ~2 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |
| Drama (res) | B=24 | ~2 per fortnight | Blank days scattered in grid |

### Day Off Constraints
- **Management cover**: At least 1 management team member must be on-site at all times.
- **Ratio compliance**: Day off allocation must not cause staffing to drop below minimum supervision ratios.
- **Staggering**: Days off should be staggered across the team.
- **Full-day excursion days**: Nearly all activity staff are assigned to FDEs. Avoid giving activity staff days off on FDE days.
- **Arrival days**: Avoid giving activity/SAI staff days off on arrival days (they handle pickups).
- **Days off are flexible**: From real rotas, days off fall on various days of the week and are not fixed to a specific weekday. They shift based on programme needs.

## 9. SAFEGUARDING RULES AFFECTING THE ROTA

### Bedtime Supervision
- **Students 10 and under**: Activities finish at 9pm, in bed by 9:30pm
- **Students 13 and under**: Activities finish at 10pm, in bed by 10:30pm
- **Students 14-17**: In bed by 11pm

### Overnight Duty
- One management team member carries the duty phone overnight (CM, CD, or EAM on rotation)

### Mealtime Supervision
- Staff are assigned "dinner" supervision slots in the rota
- From real rotas, SAIs and ALs most commonly get dinner supervision duties

### Minimum On-Site Requirements
- At least 1 First Aid qualified member on-site at all times
- At least 1 management team member on-site at all times

## 11. ROTA GENERATION RULES FOR CLAUDE CODE

When generating a rota automatically, follow these rules in order of priority:

### Priority 1: Safeguarding Compliance
- Never schedule fewer staff than the minimum supervision ratio requires
- At least 1 management team member on-site at all times
- At least 1 First Aid qualified person on-site at all times
- Minimum 2 UKLC team members on every excursion
- Duty phone assigned to a management member every night
- Class size max 16 (can stretch to 17-18 only if unavoidable)

### Priority 2: Role Constraints
- Only qualified teachers (FTT, TAL, CD) can be assigned teaching sessions
- 5-Day FTTs only work Monday-Friday, teaching sessions only
- Activity roles (AL, AC, SAI, SC, LAL, LAC) do NOT teach
- TALs can teach AND do activities (Intensive English counts as teaching)
- Football/Drama specialists deliver their specialism in activity slots
- Management (CM, CD, EAM) are "Office" by default; only override for specific duties

### Priority 3: Session Limits
- FTT: max 22 sessions/fortnight
- TAL: max 22 sessions/fortnight
- AL, AC, SAI, SC, LAL, LAC: max 24 sessions/fortnight
- Football/Drama (residential): max 24 sessions/fortnight
- HP: max 22 sessions/fortnight (~40hrs/week)
- Management: salaried (not session-counted, but track for fairness)
- Part-fortnight staff: reduce proportionally (e.g. 1 week = 11-12 sessions)

### Priority 4: Day Off Allocation
- Every residential staff member gets approximately 1 full day off per week (2 per fortnight)
- Days off are blank rows in the rota (no explicit "OFF" label needed)
- Stagger days off across the team - never have too many staff off on the same day
- Avoid giving activity staff days off on: full-day excursion days, arrival days
- FTTs can have days off on FDE days or weekends (since they don't normally do excursions)
- Management days off must be staggered

### Priority 5: Pattern Matching
Based on real rotas, follow these assignment patterns:

**FTTs**: Teaching AM, Teaching PM on most days. Testing AM+PM on post-arrival Saturday. 1-2 evening ents per fortnight. 0-1 excursion per fortnight. Day off typically falls on a day with no teaching needed.

**TALs**: Teaching some AM slots, Int Eng/Activities other PM slots. Excursion duty 2-4 times per fortnight. Evening ents 2-4 times per fortnight. More varied schedule than FTTs.

**SAIs**: Activities AM+PM, excursions, dinner supervision, welcome duties, airport pickups. Evening ents frequently. Most likely to get dinner/welcome assignments on arrival days.

**ALs**: Activities AM+PM, vlogging workshops, excursions. Evening ents frequently. Setup duties.

**Football (res)**: Football in PM activity slots. FB Excur (stadium visits) as FDE equivalents. General activities when not doing football. Evening ents.

**Drama (res)**: Drama in AM and PM activity slots. General activities and excursions. Evening ents.

**HP**: Office/admin work, excursion duty (frequently), multi-activity sessions, evening entertainment, transfer duties on departure days.

### Priority 6: Fair Distribution
- Distribute evening entertainment duties fairly across eligible staff
- Distribute airport/transfer duties fairly (SAIs and ALs get most, but share around)
- Dinner supervision should rotate among activity staff
- Don't schedule the same person for 3 sessions (AM+PM+Eve) every day

### Input Data Required
1. **Centre name** (determines London/non-London roles)
2. **Programme type**: ZZ or NZZ
3. **Staff list** with roles
4. **Programmes sheet**: each group's arrival date, departure date, nationality, student count, leader count
5. **Airport transfers sheet**: flight times, airports, dates
6. **Centre dates** (start and end)

### Output Format
The rota should be output as a 2-week spreadsheet grid:
- Column A: Staff Member name
- Column B: Sessions to work (22 for FTT/TAL/HP, 24 for activity roles, 0 for management)
- Column C: Sessions in rota (auto-counted from entries)
- Column D: Sessions left (B minus C)
- Then 3 columns per day (AM | PM | Eve) for 14 days
- Each cell contains the assignment: Teaching, Testing, Int Eng, Activities, MultiActiv, Football, Drama, Vlogging, Excursion [destination], dinner, welcome, pickup, airport, setup, [Evening Ent name], Admin, Office
- Blank cells = day off or not working that session

## 14. GLOSSARY

- **DSP**: Designated Safeguarding Person (on-site: CM and SWC)
- **DSL**: Designated Safeguarding Lead (head office)
- **ZZ**: Zig-zag programme (split AM/PM lessons and activities between 2 cohorts)
- **NZZ**: Non-zig-zag programme (all lessons AM, all activities PM)
- **FDE**: Full-day excursion (= 2 sessions, AM + PM)
- **HDE**: Half-day excursion (= 1 session, typically PM)
- **Eve Ent**: Evening Entertainment (= 1 session)
- **Int Eng**: Intensive English (extra English sessions delivered by TALs as an English Plus option)
- **FB Excur**: Football-specific excursion (e.g. stadium tour)
- **MultiActiv**: Multi-activity afternoon session
- **Session**: Approximately 3.5 hours of work
- **English Plus / Chosen Programme**: Specialist afternoon programme options (Football, Performing Arts, Intensive English, Multi-Activity, Vlogging)
- **Fortnight**: The 14-day cycle used for session counting
- **Duty Phone**: Mobile phone carried by the on-duty management member 24/7
- **Testing**: English placement test given to students on the day after arrival
- **B column**: "Sessions to work" in the rota spreadsheet
- **C column**: "Sessions in rota" (auto-counted from entries in the grid)
- **D column**: "Sessions left" (B minus C - should reach 0)
