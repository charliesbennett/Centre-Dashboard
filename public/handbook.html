What is the Centre Dashboard?
An overview of what the dashboard does and who it is for

The UKLC Centre Dashboard is the central tool for managing your summer language school programme. It brings together everything you need — student records, staff rotas, lesson timetables, catering counts, airport transfers, rooming, and more — in one place.

All changes are saved automatically and in real time. If a colleague at the same centre updates something, you will see the change the next time you load or refresh the page.

Who is it for?

Role What they use it for
Centre Manager Day-to-day operation — viewing groups, managing the rota, checking catering counts, tracking transfers, and keeping the team register up to date
Head Office Everything above, plus bulk-importing groups and staff across all centres, managing rooming data, and administering user accounts
About this handbook Sections 4–14 apply to everyone. Sections 15–17 cover Head Office-only features, which are clearly marked with a Head Office label.

Logging In
How to sign in to the dashboard

The dashboard is available at uklcdashboard.com. It runs in any modern web browser — Chrome, Firefox, Edge, and Safari are all supported. No app download is required.

Signing in

  1. Go to uklcdashboard.com in your browser.
  2. Enter your UKLC email address (e.g. yourname@uklc.org) or your username if you have been given one.
  3. Enter your password.
  4. Click Sign In.
Note Only @uklc.org email addresses are accepted. If you try to log in with a personal email address you will see an error message.
Forgotten your password? Contact Head Office to have your password reset. Your account details are managed by the UKLC admin team.

Logging out

Click your name or the sign-out button at the top right of the screen. You will be returned to the login page. Always log out when using a shared computer.

Selecting Your Centre & Finding Your Way Around
The layout of the dashboard

Selecting a centre

After signing in, the centre selector appears at the top of the screen. Click the dropdown and choose your centre. All the data shown in the tabs will be for that centre only.

If your account is linked to a specific centre, it will be pre-selected for you. Head Office users can switch between any centre at any time using the same dropdown.

The tab bar

The dashboard is divided into tabs along the top of the screen. Click any tab to switch to that section. The tabs are:

Saving your work

Most changes save automatically as you type or click. You do not need to press a Save button for everyday edits. A few areas (such as rooming assignments) have an explicit Save button — you will see it clearly on screen. In these cases, make sure to click Save before switching tab or closing your browser.

Light and dark mode

The dashboard defaults to light mode. If you prefer dark mode, click the theme toggle button at the top right of the screen. Your preference is remembered in your browser.

4
Students Tab
Group summaries, individual student records, and exports

The Students tab is your central record of all groups and students attending your centre. It has three views, selectable by the buttons at the top of the tab.

Groups view

This is the default view. Each card shows a single group with:

  • Group name and agent
  • Nationality
  • Number of students and group leaders
  • Arrival and departure dates
  • Programme type (e.g. Multi-Activity, English Plus)
  • Lesson slot (AM or PM)
  • First and last meal — used by the Catering tab to calculate meals on arrival and departure days

Click the expand arrow on any group card to see the full list of students in that group.

Adding a group manually

Click + Add Group and fill in the details. For larger imports, use the bulk import feature described in Section 15.

All Students view

A single alphabetical list of every student across all groups. Shows first name, surname, group, nationality, age, arrival and departure dates, medical notes, and room assignment. Use the search box to find a specific student by name.

Flagged view

Shows only students who have a medical condition or a dietary requirement recorded. Use this view to make sure no one has been missed before a new group arrives, and to share the list with your welfare officer and catering team.

Important — check flags before every arrival Dietary requirements such as halal, vegan, and gluten-free are automatically detected from student data. Medical notes are entered against individual students. Review the Flagged view when a new group is due.

Importing student data

If you have a VBT master Excel file with individual student records (first name, surname, group, nationality, age, medical notes, swimming ability, etc.), you can import it directly:

  1. Click the Import button at the top of the Students tab.
  2. Choose the Excel file.
  3. The dashboard will show a preview of the changes — new students added, existing ones updated.
  4. Click Confirm to apply the import.

Exporting data

Two export buttons are available:

  • Export Students — downloads an Excel file with the full student list including names, groups, ages, medical notes, and room assignments
  • Export Invoice Data — downloads an Excel file in the format required for invoicing, covering both students and group leaders with nights stayed and programme type

Rota Tab
The staff schedule grid

The Rota tab shows the full staff schedule for your centre, laid out as a grid. Each row is a staff member. Each column is a day. Each day is split into three slots: AM, PM, and Evening.

Reading the rota

Each cell shows what a staff member is doing in that slot. Common values include:

ValueMeaning
Lessons / TeachingRunning an English lesson
Activity (e.g. Football, Drama)Supervising or leading an activity session
Day OffRest day (shown in red)
Induction / SetupPreparation on arrival day
Eve ActivityRunning or supervising evening entertainment
Welcome / Pick-upMeeting groups on arrival
OfficeAdministrative duties

The fortnight selector

The programme is divided into fortnights. Use the buttons at the top to jump between fortnights. The current fortnight is highlighted.

Editing the rota

Click any cell to edit it. Type a session name or select from the dropdown that appears. Changes save automatically when you click away. To clear a cell, delete the text and click away.

Session counts and shortfalls

Summary cards at the top of the tab show:

  • Total sessions assigned in the current fortnight
  • Days off taken across the team
  • Shortfalls — slots where the required number of staff has not been assigned
Resolve shortfalls before publishing the rota A shortfall means a session does not have enough staff to meet safeguarding ratios. The rota is not ready to share until all shortfalls are cleared.
Tip — use the AI Rota Generator for a first draft If you are building the rota from scratch, the AI Rota Generator (Section 6) can produce an initial draft automatically, which you can then fine-tune manually in this tab.
6
AI Rota Generator
Automatically create a draft rota from your group and staff data

The AI Rota Generator creates a first draft of the staff rota automatically. It reads your current group numbers, lesson slots, and team data, then applies all standard UKLC rules to produce a schedule that should be correct from the start.

How it works — three steps

Step 1 — Programme

Select the fortnight you want to generate. The screen shows a summary of which groups are on site during that period and which lesson slot each group is following. Check these look correct before continuing.

Step 2 — Generate

Click Generate Rota. The system runs through every day and slot, assigns staff to sessions, places day offs, and checks all the constraints listed below. This takes a few seconds.

Step 3 — Review

The draft rota is shown as a grid. Check it over — every staff member should have at least one full day off per week, sessions should be covered, and no one should be over their session limit. If you are happy, click Copy to Rota to push the draft into the main Rota tab. If not, click Discard and generate again, or switch to the Rota tab to make manual adjustments.

Constraints automatically applied

RuleWhat it means
One session per slot per dayNo staff member is double-booked in the same slot
Day off every weekEvery staff member gets at least one full day off per seven-day period
Session limitsTALs and FTTs are capped at 22 sessions per fortnight
Safeguarding ratiosEach activity session has the minimum required number of staff
Role rulesFTTs are not assigned on excursion days; 5FTTs are not assigned at weekends
Evening coverEligible staff cover every evening entertainment session
Note The AI Rota Generator is a starting point, not a finished product. Always review the draft and make any necessary adjustments in the Rota tab before sharing it with your team.

Programmes Tab
Lesson and activity timetable for every group

The Programmes tab shows the timetable of lessons and activities for each group across the full programme period. This is the schedule that group leaders follow to know when their students are in lessons and when they are doing activities.

Reading the grid

Groups are listed as rows on the left. Dates run across the top. Within each date column there are slots (AM, PM, and Evening). Each cell shows what that group is doing in that slot on that day.

The group's lesson slot (set in the Students tab) determines whether they have lessons in the morning or afternoon. This switches between AM and PM each week automatically.

Dates that are excursion days are highlighted differently to indicate the group is off-site for part or all of the day.

Editing the timetable

Click any cell to type in an activity or lesson. Changes save automatically when you click away. Use the same names consistently (e.g. always "Football" not "football" or "Football session") so the Rota tab can match them correctly.

Tip — scroll right for the full programme If your programme is more than a week long, scroll the grid to the right to see all the dates. The group names column stays fixed on the left.
8
Catering Tab
Meal counts, dietary requirements, and kitchen sheets

The Catering tab calculates how many meals are needed each day and helps you manage dietary requirements and special catering events. The numbers are calculated automatically from the group and team data — you should not need to enter meal counts by hand.

Views

Grid

The main view: a table with meal types as rows (Breakfast, Lunch, Packed Lunch, Dinner, etc.) and dates as columns. Each number is the total for that meal on that day.

The counts are calculated from:

  • Group arrival and departure dates and their first/last meal settings
  • The number of residential staff on site
  • Full excursion days (where lunch becomes Packed Lunch)
  • Weekends (where lunch also becomes Packed Lunch)

If a number needs to be overridden, click the cell and type the correct figure.

Groups

Shows which groups are on site each day, making it easy to cross-reference the meal count with actual headcount.

Dietary

A register of students with dietary requirements. Add an entry for each student who has a special diet, specifying their name, group, type of requirement (vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, gluten-free, dairy-free, nut allergy, etc.), and any further details. This list can be printed and shared with the kitchen.

Special Meals

Record one-off events such as a birthday cake, a celebration dinner, or a themed meal night. Set the date, meal, description, and number of covers.

Offsite Meals

When a group eats off-site (for example, during an excursion), record it here so that group is deducted from the kitchen's count for that meal. Enter the group, date, which meal, and the number of people not eating on-site.

Printing a catering sheet

Click the Print button in the top toolbar to open a print-ready version of the meal grid. Print this and share it with your kitchen team at the start of each week.

Excursions Tab
Day trips, coach bookings, and weekend outings

The Excursions tab manages all day trips and weekend outings during the programme. It shows you how many students are going on each date, where they are going, and the status of any coach bookings.

The excursion list

Every day marked as a Full Day or Half Day excursion appears in this list, along with every weekend during the programme. For each excursion date you can see:

  • The date and day of the week
  • Whether it is a Full Day or Half Day excursion
  • The number of students and group leaders going
  • The destination
  • Coach details
  • Notes

Marking a day as an excursion

Excursion days are set in the Programmes tab (see Section 7). Once a day is marked there, it appears automatically in the Excursions tab.

Adding destination and coach details

  1. Find the excursion date in the list.
  2. Click the destination field and type where the group is going (e.g. "London — Tower of London").
  3. Click + Add Coach to add a coach booking.
  4. Fill in the company name, phone number, booking reference, invoice number, pick-up and drop-off times, and vehicle type.
  5. Set the status: Pending → Requested → Confirmed → Paid.

Weekend excursions

Weekends are automatically listed as Full Day excursions and shown separately in the tab. Students are always on excursion at weekends. Fill in the destination and coach details as for any other excursion day.

12
Rooming Tab
Room setup and bed assignments

The Rooming tab manages where students, group leaders, and staff sleep. It has two main views: an Overview of nightly headcounts and a Houses view for detailed room-by-room assignments.

Overview view

The overview shows a grid of houses (rows) and dates (columns). Each cell shows how many students are sleeping in that house on that night. The numbers are calculated automatically from group arrival and departure dates.

If you need to change a number for a specific night (for example, if a group is sleeping in a different house), click the cell and enter the correct figure. Overrides are shown in blue so you can distinguish them from auto-calculated values.

Houses view

The Houses view has two sub-tabs: Setup and Assign.

Setup

Define the physical layout of your accommodation:

  1. Click + Add House and enter the house name (e.g. "Windsor House").
  2. Within each house, click + Add Room to add a room. Give each room a name (e.g. "Room 12" or "Dormitory B") and a floor label.
  3. Enter the number of beds in each room — this sets its capacity.

Assign

Fill in who is sleeping in each bed. Each room shows its beds as a list. Click a bed slot and type the student or group leader's name. Rooms are colour-coded by group so you can see at a glance whether groups have been mixed.

An occupancy table at the bottom shows each house's total capacity and how many beds are currently filled.

Tip — import rooming from Excel If your rooming has already been planned in an Excel spreadsheet, Head Office can import it directly. See Section 16.

Petty Cash Tab
Income, expenses, and closing balance

The Petty Cash tab is your on-site cash book. It tracks money coming in and going out, calculates the running balance, and records the amount sent to the Regional Operations Manager (ROM) at the end of the programme.

At a glance — the summary cards

CardWhat it shows
OpeningThe amount of cash at the start of the programme
IncomeTotal money received during the programme
ExpensesTotal money paid out
To ROMAmount sent to the Regional Operations Manager
ClosingCalculated automatically: Opening + Income − Expenses − To ROM

Setting the opening balance

Enter the amount of cash you received at the start of the programme in the Opening (£) field. This is usually the float provided by Head Office.

Recording cash in

Click + Cash In to add an income entry. Select the date, enter the group or source, choose a category, and enter the amount.

Income categories: Activity, Housekeeping, Transfer, Equals Card Top-Up, Other.

Recording expenses

Click + Add Expense to add an outgoing payment. Enter the date, description, category, and amount.

Expense categories: Activities & Equipment, Cleaning, Transport, Food & Drink, Stationery, Medical, Staff Expenses, Prizes, Other.

Sending money to ROM

When you send cash to the Regional Operations Manager, enter the amount in the To ROM (£) field. This is deducted from the closing balance automatically.

Bulk Import — Groups & Staff
Head Office  Importing the master spreadsheet data

Bulk import is used at the start of summer and throughout the season to keep the dashboard in sync with the master Groups and Staff spreadsheets. It can update all centres in a single import.

How the import works

The import is intelligent and safe:

  • New groups/staff are added.
  • Existing groups/staff are updated if anything has changed in the spreadsheet.
  • Groups/staff removed from the spreadsheet are deleted from those centres. Only the centres in the import file are affected — other centres are untouched.
Important — do not select a centre before a bulk import A bulk import covers all centres in the file. Make sure the centre selector at the top of the screen shows no centre selected (blank) before you run a bulk import. If a specific centre is selected, only that centre's data will be processed.

Bulk import — Groups

  1. Ensure no centre is selected in the centre dropdown at the top of the screen.
  2. Go to the Students tab.
  3. Click the Import button and choose Bulk Import (Head Office).
  4. Select your Groups Excel file.
  5. Review the preview — it shows how many groups will be added, updated, or removed across all centres.
  6. Click Confirm to run the import.

Bulk import — Staff

  1. Ensure no centre is selected.
  2. Go to the Team tab.
  3. Click Bulk Import.
  4. Select your Staff Excel file.
  5. Click Confirm.
Run the Groups import first Staff records reference centre names. Import Groups before Staff on any given day to make sure the centre matching is accurate.

Rooming Import from Excel
Head Office  Importing a Heads-on-Beds spreadsheet

If rooming has already been planned in an Excel spreadsheet, it can be imported directly into the Rooming tab rather than entered by hand.

Supported file formats

The importer accepts Heads-on-Beds format spreadsheets. The file must have an Overview sheet (or the first sheet will be used) with this column layout:

ColumnContents
AHouse name (sparse — only shown on the first row of each house)
BFloor label (sparse — only shown on the first row of each floor)
CRoom name (e.g. "Room 1", "Dormitory B")
DBed identifier — either "Bed 1", "Bed 2"… or simply 1, 2, 3…

How to run the import

  1. Select the correct centre from the centre dropdown.
  2. Go to the Rooming tab and click the Houses view.
  3. Click Import from Excel.
  4. Choose your .xlsx or .xlsm file.
  5. The dashboard shows a preview: the number of houses, rooms, and beds detected.
  6. If the numbers look correct, click Import.
What gets imported The import creates all the house and room records in the Setup view. If the spreadsheet includes names against bed slots, those names are populated in the Assign view as well. Any existing rooming data for that centre will be replaced.
Check the preview carefully If the detected room or bed count looks wrong (too low or zero), the file may not be in the expected format. Contact the developer before proceeding.

The Users tab is only visible to Head Office accounts. It allows you to create new user accounts, assign roles and centres, and update passwords.

RoleAccess level
Head OfficeCan see and edit all centres, run bulk imports, access the Users tab

Creating a new user

  1. Go to the Users tab.
  2. Click + Add User.
  3. Enter their full name, email address, and a username (optional — used as an alternative to email at the login screen).

Common Questions & Tips
Quick answers to the most frequent questions

General

Do I need to click a Save button?

Most edits (group details, rota cells, programme cells, transfers, contacts, petty cash entries) save automatically when you click away from a field. Areas with their own Save button — such as rooming bed assignments — will show it clearly. Always click Save before navigating away from those screens.

Can two people edit the same centre at the same time?

Yes. The dashboard supports concurrent editing. Changes made by one person are synced to other users who have the same centre open. However, if two people edit the exact same cell at the same moment, the last save wins — so coordinate with your team to avoid overwriting each other.

I can see groups from another centre on my screen. What do I do?

Check the centre selector at the top of the screen. If the wrong centre is selected, click the dropdown and choose your own. If you can still see incorrect groups, contact Head Office — they can re-run the bulk import to clean up the data.

Students

A student has moved to a different group. How do I update this?

Find the student in the All Students view, click to edit their record, and change their group. If the move is reflected in the master spreadsheet, running the bulk import again will update it automatically.

The medical flag for a student is missing. Why?

Medical notes are only flagged if they have been entered against the individual student record (not the group). Check the student's record in the expanded group view and make sure the Medical field has been filled in.

Rota & Team

A staff member isn't appearing on the rota. Why?

Check their arrival and departure dates in the Team tab. If their dates don't cover the period you are looking at, they won't appear on the rota for those days. Also check they haven't been accidentally deleted — if so, add them again or re-run the staff bulk import.

The AI Rota generated too many day offs. What should I do?

The AI follows the contracted minimum (one day off per week). If your contract requires more, or if specific staff have different arrangements, adjust the day offs manually in the Rota tab after generating the draft.

Catering

The meal counts don't look right. What should I check?

The most common causes are: (1) a group's arrival or departure date is wrong in the Students tab — correct it there and the catering grid will update automatically; (2) the first or last meal setting is wrong — check the group card in the Students tab; (3) an excursion day hasn't been marked as a Full Day excursion in the Programmes tab.

Rooming

I've entered some bed assignments but they disappeared. What happened?

The rooming Assign view has a Save button. If you navigated away without clicking Save, your changes were not stored. Always click Save after entering bed assignments.

Getting help

For issues not covered in this handbook, contact UKLC Head Office. If you believe you have found a bug in the dashboard, note what you were doing when the issue occurred and contact the admin team with as much detail as possible.
